What's inside

Everything a modern EQ should do

Eight bands, full Mid/Side routing, and the parts that usually take the longest — finding and taming resonances — done automatically.

M/S

True Mid/Side EQ

Every band works on Mid, Side, or both — tighten the mono center without narrowing your stereo image, or the reverse.

RES

Live resonance detection

A psychoacoustic detector runs constantly in the background, flagging sustained problem frequencies with live labels — mid in green, side in orange.

FR

Find Resonances assistant

Listens for 8 seconds and proposes up to 6 dynamic EQ bands automatically — one click to apply, one click to undo.

DYN

Per-band dynamic EQ

Any band can become dynamic — threshold, range, attack, and release — so it only cuts when a frequency actually becomes a problem.

HP/LP

Independent Mid/Side filters

Run high-pass and low-pass at completely different frequencies for Mid and Side — keep your low end mono and tight without touching the center.

↺

5-step undo/redo

Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y across everything — manual edits, applied proposals, preset changes. Nothing is ever a risk to try.

A/B

A / B / C / D snapshots

Four full settings slots to flip between instantly, so you can compare decisions instead of guessing from memory.

CTRL

Ctrl+hover audition

Hold Ctrl and hover any band to solo just that frequency range — hear exactly what you're about to touch before you touch it.

⚠

Mono-compatibility warning

Get warned when your side channel builds up too much low-end energy — a common cause of phase cancellation on mono playback.

The centerpiece

Stop hunting for problem frequencies by ear.

Find Resonances listens to your track and does the part that usually takes ten minutes with a narrow bell and a good pair of ears — in eight seconds.

1

Click Find Resonances, play your track

It listens for 8 seconds while your mix plays.

2

It measures, not just detects

Bandwidth sets the Q, prominence sets the cut, decay time sets attack/release — automatically, per resonance.

3

Review the proposal, apply or discard

Up to 6 dynamic bands appear as chips. Apply with one click, or undo the whole thing with another.

4

Bands only engage when needed

They're dynamic, not static cuts — silent until that frequency actually becomes a problem.

Why Mid/Side matters

Two channels most EQs can't see.

A normal stereo EQ treats left and right the same. MSEQ 8 splits your signal into Mid (the mono center) and Side (everything that makes it stereo) — so you can fix one without touching the other.

Mid

The mono center of your mix — usually where lead vocals, bass, and kick live. Tighten it up without narrowing your stereo image at all.

Side

Everything that makes your mix stereo — reverbs, width, air. Shape it independently, or high-pass it harder than the center to keep bass mono.
